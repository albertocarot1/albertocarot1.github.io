## Lessons Learned

### What Went Well âœ“
- Quad-tracking rhythm guitars with different amp tones created massive wall of sound
- Pre-production demos in Guitar Pro saved 2+ weeks of studio time
- Vocal comping across 5+ takes yielded professional results
- Investing in quality DI signals paid off during re-amping phase

### Challenges Faced âš 
- Initial drum takes had timing issues, required extensive editing
- Bass tone struggled to sit in mix until trying different EQ approach (cutting 400Hz mud)
- Vocal tracking took longer than expected due to ambitious melodies
- Mix translation issues between studio monitors and consumer devices

### Key Takeaways ðŸ’¡
- Always record DI and amped signals simultaneously for flexibility
- Reference tracks are essential - A/B comparison throughout mixing prevented frequency buildup
- Vocal production is 50% performance, 50% editing/processing
- Mixing breaks every 45 minutes improved decision-making and ear fatigue

### Future Improvements
- Invest in better monitoring setup (room treatment + calibrated speakers)
- Track bass with multiple tones (clean, distorted) for mixing options
- Learn advanced drum editing techniques to speed up post-production
- Collaborate with experienced producer for next release to level up production quality
